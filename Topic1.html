HTTP History & Early Versions

HTTP (Hypertext Transfer Protocol) allows browsers and servers to communicate. It was created in the late 1980s and early 1990s by Tim Berners-Lee at CERN.

The first version, HTTP/0.9 (1991), was simple and only handled text. HTTP/1.0 (1996) added headers, supported images and videos, and introduced error codes like 404 Not Found.
